📋 The Three SHA Funds
1. Primary Healthcare Fund (PHF)
Government-funded. Available to all registered SHA members at no extra cost.
Facilities coveredLevel 2–3 (clinics, health centres)
Annual capitationKsh 900 per person/year
Visits allowedUnlimited within covered services
CoversDoctor visits, basic tests, chronic disease management
2. Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF)
Funded by 2.75% of gross salary (minimum Ksh 300/month).
Facilities coveredLevel 4–6 hospitals
ServicesInpatient, outpatient, surgical, radiology, dialysis, oncology
Outpatient cap (Level 3–4)Ksh 2,500 per visit
Consultation fee (average)~Ksh 2,000 (nearly exhausts cap)
3. Emergency, Chronic & Critical Illness Fund (ECCIF)
Activated only when SHIF benefits are fully depleted.
CoversEmergencies, severe and chronic conditions
ActivationAfter SHIF exhausted
🎗️ Oncology Coverage
Diagnostic tests (annual)Ksh 100,000
Cancer treatment (annual)Ksh 300,000
Total oncology (annual)Ksh 400,000
Treatment abroad (max)Ksh 500,000/year
PET & PSMA scansFully covered
ℹ️ SHA covers up to Ksh 300,000 for cancer treatment annually — vs NHIF which capped at 6 sessions only. This is a significant improvement for oncology patients.
🏛️ Civil Servant Coverage
Inpatient cover (annual)Ksh 2.5M–2.8M (by job group)
Outpatient limit (annual)Ksh 250,000–300,000
Dental & opticalUp to Ksh 50,000/year
Per-visit outpatient capKsh 2,500
Diagnosis-specific capYes — limits apply per condition
⚠️ Critical for civil servants: Even if your overall inpatient balance shows available funds, a specific condition may be capped (e.g. Ksh 600,000 per diagnosis). Once that ceiling is reached, you pay out of pocket — even with balance remaining. Always ask the hospital about diagnosis-specific limits before admission.
